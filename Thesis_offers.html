Master's thesis topics

  • "Systematic-aware DNN training"
    Systematic effect are in many cases the leading source of uncertainties for measurement of processes with large cross-sections predicted by the standard model. The classic approach to enhance data analysis sensitivity using machine learning does not directly tackle this fact, since often the target is to minimize the cross-entropy in each class. A different paradigm has been developed at KIT, which includes in the loss function the minimization of the expected uncertainty on the final result, instead. We propose to apply this approach on a well-known process, like the production of W boson pairs at the LHC, and compare the results with those currently quoted by CMS.
    Skills: W boson physics, data analysis, Python programming, C++ programming, ROOT, machine learning.

Bachelor's thesis topics

  • "WH charge asymmetry"
    The production of a Higgs boson in association with a W boson is sensitive to the coupling of the Higgs boson to light quarks. Anomalous couplings may in fact influence the production of positive or negative W bosons, on top of the asymmetry introduced by the PDFs. We are analyzing CMS data to target such measurement, focusing on the Higgs boson decay to W boson pairs. The analysis is set up and under review in CMS. We plan to conclude it, and then to combine it with the same analysis targeting the Higgs boson decay to a pair of tau leptons, also developed at KIT.
    Skills: Higgs-boson physics, data analysis, Python programming, C++ programming, ROOT, statistical analysis.
